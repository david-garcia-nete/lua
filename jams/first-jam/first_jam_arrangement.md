# First Jam Arrangement

## Global Settings
- **Key:** A minor
- **Tempo:** 80 BPM
- **Time signature:** 4/4
- **Style / mood:** Mid-tempo cinematic pop/rock jam; starts minimal, builds to full-energy hook sections, breathes in the bridge, then lands in a soft outro.

## Practice Notes
- **What to practice on piano**
  - Lock LH roots/fifths with steady whole-bar sustain while RH triads stay even and quiet in C-01/C-04.
  - Rehearse quick register shift between C-01 and C-03 without changing chord spelling.
  - For melody, phrase the 2-note cells legato and keep octave doubles lighter than the main line.

- **What to practice on guitar**
  - Map Am–F–C–G and F–G–Am–Am as consistent 4-bar loops; use muted strums in low-energy sections.
  - Increase strum density and brightness in pre-drop/final chorus to mirror chord velocity changes.
  - Practice dynamic contrast: sparse bridge vs. full final chorus.

- **What to practice on bass**
  - Nail subdivision differences between sparse (half notes), normal (2+1+1 beats), and active (quarters) patterns.
  - Keep note length controlled so kick transients remain clear.
  - In active sections, maintain identical articulation through chord changes to keep momentum.

- **What to listen for in Ardour**
  - Energy contour across 56 bars: intro build, chorus peaks, bridge dip, final peak, outro release.
  - Drum fill transitions at bars 32, 36, 52 should clearly signal form changes.
  - Melody octave doubles should add width, not overpower the lead voice.
  - Low-end balance: kick and bass should interlock, especially in active bass sections.
